Roger Federer

A legendary tennis player

Here's a timeline of Federer's life:

  • 1981 - Born in Basel, Switzerland
  • 1996 - Plays his first junior tennis match at a grade 2 tournament in Switzerland
  • 1998 - Becomes No. 1 in the junior world ranking and International Tennis Federation (ITF) junior World Champion
  • 2002 - Wins his first Master Series at the 2002 Hamburg Masters which ranks him as one of the top tennis players in the world
  • 2003 - Wins his first Grand Slam singles title at Wimbledon
  • 2004 - Becomes world ranked number 1
  • 2006 - Wins three of the four Grand Slam tournaments, losing to only two players
  • 2007 - Wins his tenth Grand Slam singles title, the Australian Open, becoming the first man to win a Grand Slam title without dropping a set since Bjorn Borg at the French Open in 1980
  • 2008 - Wins the fourth consecutive Laureus World Sportsman of the Year award
  • 2009 - Present - Proceeds to amaze his fans with his talent

"the remarkable part is that 20 years later, Federer still knows how to win matches on Centre Court, even if winning seven of them in a row sounds like too much to ask."

-- Bjorn Borg, 11-time grand slam winner
